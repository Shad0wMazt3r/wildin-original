Wildin' 3.0

The third installment of Wildin was a milestone event as it is one of the biggest Hip-Hop events to happen in Delhi post-lockdown. As the world opens up and starts getting back to the old groove, events and artists have returned to take back the nights. Wildin was an attempt to do the same and was a successful one too as more than 250 people showed up. Almost 90% of the tickets were sold in the early-bird booking phase and little to no booking slots were available on the day of the event. The audience that turned up was impressive as well, given how versatile they were. The event hosted rap performances, rap battles, beatboxing battles, and pop-up stalls. Artists from Mumbai, Delhi, and Jamshedpur came together to make this event an absolute blast. From the breathtaking rap battles to amazing performances and beautiful art that was up for sale, this is probably the first time in Delhi where such different artists joined hands to create this one-of-a-kind experience. While the stalls has people buying art, getting tattoos, and clicking photos, the rap performances had them jumping and grooving . Young kings of Indian Hip-Hop like Gravity, Siege, Farhan, and D-cypher owned the night were their on-stage presence and songs that left the audience wanting for more. On the other hand, visual artists like Sharon, Ramaa, and Vishaka had people lining up at their stalls. The beatboxing and rap battles were a whole different story in themselves. The audience went from focusing intensely on the build-ups to screaming and cheering on drops and punchlines. Rapture, the battle rap veteran from Mumbai showed how experience and delivery can woo the audience regardless of the city you step in. Dzire, the battle rap prodigy from Jamshedpur was the highlight of the event as she made her mark in the capital city and proved that she is a force to be reckoned with. Delhi artists also held their own at the event as acts like Pho, Oof, YKSGOD, and Shinigxmi had people singing along to their beats. The organizer of this event, Abhijeet Singh, popularly known as Stitch is inspired and excited more than ever to curate more such events and offer new and upcoming artists a stage that they deserve.
